TIMELINE

  • December 9, 1906: Born in New York
  • 1928: Graduated Vassar college
  • 1930: Earned Master's Degree
  • 1934: Achieved Ph.D
  • 1943: Enlisted in the Navy
  • 1944: assigned to Harvard University's Bureau of Ships Computation Project
  • 1952: Along with her team created the first complier for computers
  • 1954: Promoted to First Director of Automatic Programming
  • 1959: As Technical Advisor of CODASYL, was able to assist in the defining of COBOL
  • 1966: Retires at the age of 60, but was asked to return to active duty next year in August
  • 1967: Promoted to Navy Director in the Navy Programming Languages Group in the Navy's Office of Information Systems Planning
  • 1971: Retired again but because of her amazing skills, was asked to return to active duty yet again in 1972
  • 1973: Promoted to Captain
  • 1986: Forced to retire at the age of 79 with the rank of Commodore, now known as Rear Admiral
  • January 1, 1992: Grace Hopper passed away in Arlington, Virginia at the age of 85

DID YOU KNOW?

  • Grace Hopper became the first woman to earn a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale University in 1934.
  • In 1952, Grace Hopper and her team created the first compiler for computer languages.
  • Grace Hopper became the first female individual recipient of the National Medal of Technology in 1991.
  • When she retired from the U.S. Navy in 1986, at age 79, Grace Hopper was the oldest serving officer in the service.
  • The USS Hopper, a naval ship that was commissioned in 1997, was named in Grace Hopper's honor.
  • Grace Hopper appeared on television with David Letterman in 1986, during which she explained nanoseconds and picoseconds to her host.
